Welcome to the PlayPrep Beta 👋

PlayPrep is an activity planner built for parents, teachers, and homeschoolers. Think of it as a recipe manager for kids' activities — save crafts, field trips, and learning activities, track your supplies, and plan your week all in one place.

This is an early beta. Most core features are working, and we want to know what feels great, what's confusing, and what's missing. There are no wrong answers — your honest reaction is exactly what we need.

How long will testing take? As little as 15 minutes to try the basics, or as long as a week if you want to really put it through its paces. Both are helpful.

💬 Join our Facebook beta group to share feedback, ask questions, and connect with other testers.

Getting started

First steps

🚀 Onboarding

When you first open the app, you'll go through a short onboarding flow that asks about the ages of your kids and your interests. This helps PlayPrep suggest relevant activities to get you started.

  • Does onboarding feel smooth and quick?
  • Are the age group and interest options relevant to you?
  • After onboarding, does the home screen feel personalized and useful?

Core features to test

What to try

📚 PlayPrep Library & My Library

Browse 50+ curated activities in the PlayPrep Library (tap Discover → PlayPrep Library). Add activities you like to your personal library to save, edit, and customize them.

  • Browse the library — do the activities feel age-appropriate and useful?
  • Try filtering by age group, category, environment, and prep time
  • Add 2–3 activities to your library using the + button
  • Open an activity you saved — tap "Edit" and change the title, age group, or add a supply
  • Add a cover photo to one of your activities
  • Does the activity card in your library update to show your changes?

➕ Add Your Own Activity

Tap the center + button → Add Activity to create your own activity from scratch. You can enter it manually or (on Pro) import from a URL.

  • Create a simple activity manually — add a title, category, age group, and 2–3 steps
  • Add supplies to the activity
  • Add a cover photo
  • Save it — does it appear correctly in your library?
  • Go back and edit it — do your changes save?

🧺 Supply Closet & Shopping List

The Supply Closet tracks craft supplies you have at home. PlayPrep uses it to tell you which activities you can do right now.

  • Add 5–10 supplies to your closet (tap the Supplies tab → + Add Supply)
  • Go back to the Library tab — does it show "Ready now" activities that match your supplies?
  • From an activity, tap the supply toggle to see which supplies you have or need
  • Add missing supplies to your shopping list
  • Open the shopping list (Plan tab → Shopping List) — are items listed correctly?
  • Check off items in the shopping list — does the "Add to Closet" option work?

▶️ Play Mode

When you're ready to do an activity, tap "Play Mode" on the activity detail screen for step-by-step guidance.

  • Open any activity and tap Play Mode
  • Step through all the instructions — are they easy to follow?
  • Does the supply list appear at the right step?
  • Try logging the activity at the end — does it ask about supply usage?

📅 Planning

The Plan tab lets you schedule activities for specific days and see your upcoming week.

  • Add an activity to today's date in the Plan tab
  • Go to the Home screen — does it appear in the "On Your Plan" section?
  • Add something for tomorrow too
  • Try the shopping list view in the Plan tab

🗂️ Collections

Collections let you group activities by theme, season, skill, or any other way that makes sense to you.

  • Create a new collection (tap + → Add Collection)
  • Add 3–4 activities to it
  • Check the smart collections in the Collections tab — do they populate with relevant activities?
  • Try saving a smart collection to your library

🗺️ Places

Save museums, parks, nature centers, and anywhere else you love to take kids.

  • Add a place (tap + → Add Place)
  • Mark its status: want to visit, visited, or favorite
  • Add some details: hours, website, age range
  • Does the place card look right in your library?

Things we especially want to know

Focus areas

🤔 Overall clarity

  • Did you understand what the app does within the first 2 minutes?
  • Was there anything you couldn't figure out without help?
  • Did any labels, buttons, or flows feel confusing or misnamed?
  • Is there anything you expected to find that wasn't there?

💾 Data persistence

  • Close the app completely and reopen it — is all your data still there?
  • Edit an activity, leave the app, come back — are your edits saved?
  • Add a cover photo — does it still show after closing and reopening?

📱 Performance & feel

  • Does the app feel fast and responsive?
  • Any screens that are slow to load?
  • Any crashes or unexpected behavior?
  • Does it feel polished enough to recommend to a friend?

⚠️ Known limitations in this beta

  • URL import (paste a link to auto-fill an activity) is a Pro feature — not available in the free tier yet
  • Cloud backup and sync is not yet available — all data is stored locally on your device
  • Map view for Places is a Pro feature coming soon
  • Some settings screens show "coming soon" features that are in development
  • Pro subscription purchasing is not yet active — everything is free during the beta
